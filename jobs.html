Job Openings

"We’re hiring! Be part of something bigger and grow with us."

Whether you’re an experienced VFX artist or just starting your journey, we offer a range of positions across various departments:

Paint and Roto Artists:

At connectlifevfx, we are a leading visual effects studio dedicated to bringing imagination to life. We work with global clients to deliver high-quality post-production and VFX services for film, television, and commercials. Join our world-class team and contribute to creating visually stunning effects for top-tier projects.


Compositors


We are seeking a talented Compositor to join our creative team. As a compositor, you will play a key role in crafting the final, polished shots that captivate audiences by seamlessly blending live-action footage with computer-generated imagery (CGI). You’ll work closely with our VFX artists and filmmakers to deliver visually stunning and believable scenes that elevate our projects to the next level.


Matchmove Artists


As a Matchmove Artist at connectlifevfx, you will be an integral part of our VFX team, working closely with animators and visual effects artists to ensure the accurate tracking and match-moving of shots in dynamic scenes. Your expertise in matchmoving will play a critical role in creating visually stunning and believable compositions that captivate audiences. You will be responsible for ensuring that CG elements align perfectly with live-action footage, contributing to the overall realism and quality of our projects.


3D Artists

As a 3D Artist, you will model, texture, and animate characters, environments, and visual elements, transforming creative concepts into captivating visual stories. Working closely with design and production teams, you'll ensure that every element, from characters to backgrounds, aligns with our clients' vision. Your role combines artistic skill with technical expertise, bringing imagination to life in immersive and engaging ways that elevate storytelling across projects.


FX Artists

As an FX Artist, you will simulate and create stunning effects such as fire, water, destruction, and magical phenomena for feature films, TV series, and commercials. Your expertise in visual effects will be crucial in enhancing the narrative and emotional impact of each project. Working collaboratively with directors, animators, and other artists, you will craft dynamic, immersive visuals that captivate audiences and elevate the storytelling experience. Your innovative approach will push the boundaries of what is possible in visual effects, making every scene come alive with breathtaking realism and artistry.


Production Coordinators

Production Coordinators play a pivotal role in the filmmaking, television, and animation industries, serving as the backbone of the production process. They are responsible for overseeing the logistical aspects of a project, ensuring that workflows are streamlined, deadlines are met, and communication flows smoothly across various departments. By managing these elements effectively, Production Coordinators contribute to the overall success of a project, from its initial concept to final delivery.


VFX QC (Quality Control)

The VFX QC Specialist plays a critical role in ensuring the highest quality standards in visual effects production. Responsible for meticulously reviewing, testing, and validating visual effects content, the VFX QC Specialist identifies technical and visual inconsistencies to uphold studio and client expectations. This role requires an eye for detail, strong technical knowledge of VFX processes, and a deep understanding of production quality standards.


Team Leads

As a VFX Team Leader and Mentor, your primary responsibility is to foster a collaborative and efficient environment across multiple departments within the VFX production pipeline. You’ll lead and guide artists through technical and creative challenges, providing support, feedback, and expertise to ensure their work aligns with the project’s vision and meets quality standards. This involves a hands-on approach to mentoring team members, from junior artists to senior-level staff, helping them to develop their skills and grow professionally.



Line Producers

In this role, you’ll coordinate all aspects of VFX production, from tracking progress to balancing priorities, ensuring that each project phase aligns with the overall schedule and budget constraints. This includes forecasting resource needs, identifying potential bottlenecks, and making real-time adjustments to keep the project moving forward efficiently. By serving as the central point of communication, you’ll bridge the gap between production and creative departments, facilitating feedback, managing revisions, and ensuring that everyone has the information they need to meet the project’s artistic and technical goals.
